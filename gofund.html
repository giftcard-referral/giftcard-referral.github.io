My name is Ari. I'm an Americorps volunteer this year and serving LGBTQ+ youth in Western MA. I'm a Puerto Rican woman who is transgender, and quite frankly, it's amazing that I've managed to secure housing, finances, and a meaningful role in my community, bringing after school programming to marginalized youth.

Unfortunately, I am not okay. A roommate I accepted into my home this past March took advantage of our state's moratorium and decided to legally freeload here until the lease was up, leaving me with his debt and disappearing from the apartment. I had to pay $2850 out of pocket to make up for his rent and the mess he left behind.

In light of the pandemic's rise in February, I spent months saving up and preparing for the winter. I paid my rent on time and saved up enough to pay for my own lease in September, a whopping $3,000 put aside to secure housing. Because I was left with my roommate's debt, I was legally responsible for it, especially if I wanted to renew my lease.

I'm genuinely sorry to crowdfund. No, I'm not self-deprecating. It's simply that I spent a lot of my year saving money and donating to others. It wasn't in my range of expectations that the same cisgender, white roommate who was transcribing city council meetings to #DefundThePolice would also leave me with a mountain of debt and collect money from me for utilities he never paid. I need to reach my goal to maintain my good standing as a tenant.

Thank you in advance to everyone who donates. This is a painful lesson, but I'm only thriving because of the kindness others shared with me. In the same way I am there for others when I have abundance, I am thankful for those in my community who lift me up and help me during these types of emergencies. Seriously, thank you. You're awesome!!

-Ari
